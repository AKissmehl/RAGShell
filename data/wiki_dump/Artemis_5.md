## Original Overview

Artemis V is planned to be the fourth crewed mission and the second lunar landing of the NASA-led Artemis program. The mission is expected to mark the first efforts by NASA to begin building a permanent Moon base. As of March 2026, the launch is scheduled for late 2028.

Originally, Artemis V was proposed as the program's third lunar landing mission, sending an Orion spacecraft with four astronauts to the now-cancelled Lunar Gateway space station, where they would install two elements before heading to the lunar surface.

### Original Artemis V Lander Mission Plan

Two astronauts will board the Blue Moon lunar lander and descend to the lunar south pole, landing near the Lunar Terrain Vehicle. This mission will be the first lunar landing since Apollo 17 to utilize an unpressurized lunar rover. The two astronauts are planned to spend approximately one week on the lunar surface, conducting science and exploration activities.

Prior to February 2026, Artemis V was scheduled to launch no earlier than March 2030.

## Spacecraft

### Space Launch System

[Main article: Space Launch System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Launch_System)

The Space Launch System (SLS) is a super-heavy-lift launcher used to launch the Orion spacecraft from Earth to a trans-lunar orbit.

### Orion

[Main article: Orion (spacecraft)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orion_(spacecraft))

Orion is the crew transport vehicle used by all Artemis missions. It will transport the crew from Earth to lunar orbit and return them to Earth.

### Lunar Gateway

[Main article: Lunar Gateway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar_Gateway)

The Lunar Gateway was a small modular space station planned to be established in a Near-Rectilinear Halo Orbit (NRHO) in December 2027. However, the project was cancelled in early 2026.

### Blue Origin's Blue Moon Lander

[Main article: Blue Moon (spacecraft)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blue_Moon_(spacecraft))

The Blue Moon lander is designed to transfer astronauts from the now-cancelled Gateway to the lunar surface and back. Blue Origin is the second provider contracted by NASA to deliver Artemis astronauts to the lunar surface, following SpaceX's Starship Human Landing System.

Blue Origin will design, develop, test, and verify its Blue Moon lander to meet NASA's requirements for recurring astronaut expeditions to the lunar surface. The contract includes one uncrewed demonstration mission to the lunar surface and a crewed demonstration mission in 2030. The total award value of the contract is $3.4 billion.

### Lunar Terrain Vehicle

[Main article: Lunar Terrain Vehicle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar_Terrain_Vehicle)

The Lunar Terrain Vehicle (LTV) is an unenclosed rover being developed by NASA for astronauts to drive on the Moon while wearing spacesuits.
